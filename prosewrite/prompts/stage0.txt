Produce a structured critique covering:

1. PREMISE STRENGTH — Is the core conflict original, specific, and sustainable for a full novel? What makes it potentially compelling or generic?

2. PLOT LOGIC — Are there obvious holes, coincidences, or leaps of logic that will require later contortions to resolve?

3. GENRE FIT — Does the premise match its apparent genre conventions? What genre expectations must it meet (or knowingly subvert)?

4. CHARACTER POTENTIAL — Do the described characters have enough internal contradiction and want/need tension to drive a full story?

5. WORLD COHERENCE — Are the rules of the world (technology, politics, society) internally consistent? Where are the gaps?

6. SCOPE — Is this premise sized correctly for a novel? Too small (short story territory)? Too large (needs a trilogy)?

7. HOOKS — What are the most compelling elements that must be preserved and amplified? What is the single best thing about this premise?

8. RECOMMENDATIONS — Concrete, actionable suggestions to strengthen the premise before proceeding. Be specific. "Add more conflict" is not useful. "The antagonist's motivation is currently indistinguishable from the protagonist's — they need opposing philosophies, not just opposing methods" is useful.

Write your analysis in clear, direct prose. Do not hedge. Do not praise unless the praise is specific and earned.
