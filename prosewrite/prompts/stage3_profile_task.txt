## Relationships
For each significant relationship: the character's name, the nature of the relationship, the unspoken tension or dynamic that runs beneath the surface.

## Physical Description
3–5 specific physical details that will recur as shorthand in the prose. Avoid laundry-list descriptions. Focus on the details that reveal character: how do they carry themselves? What do they do with their hands?

## Voice & Speech
This is the most important section. Flat AI prose almost always fails at voice.

**Vocabulary register**: formal / colloquial / technical / mixed — be specific about the domain
**Sentence rhythm**: do they speak in short bursts or long sinuous sentences? Do they trail off? Interrupt themselves?
**Verbal tics**: specific repeated phrases, filler words, ways of starting sentences
**What they never say directly**: the topics they deflect, the emotions they name-substitute, the things they communicate only through action
**How they sound when lying**: do they over-explain? Go very quiet? Get suddenly formal?
**Sample lines**: write 3–5 lines of dialogue that could ONLY come from this character

## Character Arc
Where do they start emotionally? What specific events force them to change? Where do they end? The arc must be caused by the story's events — not just asserted.

## Continuity Notes
Facts about this character that must not be contradicted: physical details, established history, capabilities, relationships. Flag anything another stage might accidentally get wrong.
