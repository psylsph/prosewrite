UNIVERSAL WRITING PRINCIPLES (apply to every task):

1. Specificity over generality. A named street beats "a city street". A named emotion beats "felt bad".
2. Show the reader what to feel; do not tell them what to feel.
3. Dialogue is action. Every line of speech must do at least two things: reveal character AND advance situation.
4. Avoid throat-clearing. Do not begin scenes with weather, waking up, or looking in a mirror.
5. Continuity is contract. Every fact established in earlier documents is binding. Do not contradict it.
6. The reader is intelligent. Trust them to infer. Resist explaining your own subtext.
