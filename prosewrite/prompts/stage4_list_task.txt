## ANTI-OVERLAP RULES ⚠️⚠️⚠️

You MUST ensure each chapter is DISTINCT. Apply these rules:

1. **SAME LOCATION = MERGE**: If two chapters both occur in the same location with similar characters, MERGE them into one chapter with both beats.

2. **SAME CHARACTER ARC = MERGE**: If two chapters both focus on the same character's development of the same trait/relationship, MERGE them.

3. **SAME PLOT BEAT = MERGE**: If two chapters advance the same plot thread in similar ways (e.g., two "they escape" chapters), MERGE them.

4. **SEQUENCE BEATS NOT STATES**: A chapter should move the plot FORWARD, not just show characters in a state. "Character arrives at location" is NOT enough — something must CHANGE.

5. **HOOKS MUST DIFFER**: Chapter 1's hook cannot be "character is in X" if Chapter 2's hook is also "character is in X" but slightly later. The hook must represent a shift, not a continuation.

6. **ONE CHAPTER PER MAJOR BEAT**: Each major story beat (revelation, decision, failure, success, death, escape, etc.) should appear in ONE chapter only.

7. **NO REPETITIVE STRUCTURE**: Don't do "they plan, they execute" back-to-back repeatedly. Vary: plan-execute-reveal, then conflict-consequence-decision, then setup-tension-reversal.

⚠ EXAMPLES OF OVERLAP (DO NOT DO THIS):
- Chapter 5: "Team discusses mission, decides to proceed" + Chapter 6: "Team executes mission" = OVERLAP, MERGE
- Chapter 12: "Maria confronts Jack about his past" + Chapter 13: "Maria and Jack argue about trust" = OVERLAP (same relationship beat), MERGE
- Chapter 18: "They escape the burning building" + Chapter 19: "They escape the pursuing soldiers" = OVERLAP (escape repeated twice), MERGE

⚠ BE BRUTAL: 25 DISTINCT chapters is better than 35 chapters with overlap. If you're unsure whether two chapters are distinct, they probably aren't — MERGE them.
