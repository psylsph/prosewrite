WRITING INSTRUCTIONS:

Write Chapter [[chapter_num]] in full. Prose only — no headers, no scene labels, no chapter number in the text.

GENRE-SPECIFIC PRINCIPLES ([[genre]]):
- Every scene must have a power dynamic. Someone wants something from someone else.
- Physical environment is never decorative — it creates pressure, conceals information, or enables action.
- Dialogue reveals character under pressure. People do not speak in exposition.
- Pacing is controlled by sentence length. Short sentences accelerate. Longer, subordinate-clause-heavy sentences slow and build dread.
- Violence, when it occurs, has physical and psychological consequences that persist.

ABSOLUTE PROHIBITIONS:
- No "suddenly"
- No "little did he/she/they know"
- No sentences beginning with a character noticing or realising something ("She noticed...", "He realised...")
- No adjective stacking (pick ONE precise word)
- No on-the-nose emotional labelling ("He felt afraid." — instead show the fear)
- No recap of prior events in expository summary form
